Joy Buolamwini

MIT A.I. Researcher

For years, Joy has been researching Artificial intelligence. Buolamwini studied computer science at Georgia Institute of Technology and has earned a Master's Degree from MIT. Her MIT thesis methodology uncovered large racial and gender bias in AI services. She has hosted Ted Talks on these topics and has been named one of Forbes Top 50 Women in Tech, MIT Tech Review 35 under 35 2018 Honoree and a Rhodes Scholar. She founded the Algorithmic Justice League

Cathy O’Neil

Data Science Researcher and Author

Cathy O'Neill received her undergraduate degree from the University of Califorinia Berkley and a Ph.D. from Harvard University in mathematics. She work in the mathematics departments of Barnard College and MIT. Most notably, O'Neill published her book Weapons of Math Destruction about the societal impact of algorithms how some big data algorithms are increasingly used in ways that reinforce preexisting inequality and was awarded the Euler Book Prize.

Dr. Nicol Turner Lee

The director of the Center for Technology Innovation of TechTank

Dr. Turner Lee is a senior fellow in Governance Studies at TechTank. She received her BA from Colgate University and her PhD from Northwestern University. She has also previously served as vice president and the first director of the Media and Technology Institute at the Joint Center for Political and Economic Studies. Dr. Turner Lee has been cited in the New York Times, Washington Post, San Francisco Chronicle, Communications Daily, Multichannel News, and Washington Informer. She can also be seen or heard on NPR, NBC News, ABC and other print and online publications. She is also a widely sought expert and speaker on issues related to communications policies in media and at conferences, and she has testified on multiple occasions before Congress.

Clare Garvie

Senior Associate at Center on Privacy & Technology at Georgetown Law

Clare Garvie graduated from Georgetown Law in 2015. She was lead author on three of the Georgetown Law's Center’s reports on face recognition, including: The Perpetual Line-Up: Unregulated Police Face Recognition in America in 2016; and Garbage In, Garbage Out: Face Recognition on Flawed Data and America Under Watch: Face Surveillance in the United States in 2019. In 2019 she also testified before the House Oversight Committee about police use of face recognition. Her commentary has appeared in The New York Times, The Washington Post, and The Wall Street Journal.
